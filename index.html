Who am I?

Hi! My name is Luiza and I'm just another person trying to deal with a world pandemic while being at home. For this I started making sourdough bread at home.

This is a register of my journey and a bit of what I learned. Please don't take this as a tutorial or an expert's view, I'm not an expert. You can check content from people that really understand about bread linked on the right. You can see bread I made here.

That said, I have to mention the two most important things I've learned making bread:

  1. The more you do it, the better results you get. There is always room for improvement and practice will make you understand the process more and more.
    Luckily, as long as you don't screw up to much, even a flat, boring bread will taste really good because it already is a thousand times better than a store-bought bread, for the quality of the ingredients and because you made it.

  2. Observation is your best tool. Once you start to understand the variables and how they affect the result of your bread, you'll use a recipe as a parameter, but you'll watch each step carelfully knowing what to expect and seeing how your dough is reacting.
    This is a game changer.

How to make sourdough bread?

Making sourdough bread has a process, and it took me a while to understand it. Here are the main steps:

  1. Feed the starter to build the levain
  2. Mix ingredients or autolyse
  3. Bulk fermentation
  4. Pre-shape
  5. Final shape and proofing
  6. Bake

In more detail:

Step Description
Feed the starter You should use your starter when it's at its peak (levain), so feed it and wait the number of necessary hours for it to rise. Use it before it falls.
Mix ingredients or autolyse Mix flour + water (autolyse) and wait about one hour before adding the starter and the salt. Or just mix everything together from the beginning.
Bulk fermentation You should fold the dough every 2 hours or so to build strenght, and observe it. Do it until it grows 50%. This highly depends on temperature on your kitchen + your starter.
Pre-shape Do one set of folding and leave in covered for 30 minutes before taking it for proofing. This step is optional.
Final shape and proofing Do one last set of folding and put the dough on the banneton. Let it rest in room temperature for a few hours or overnight in the fridge.
Score and Bake Make a cut of about 1cm deep on your dough and bake it covered (on a dutch oven / cast iron pan) for 25 min. Finish baking uncovered until it's very brown.
Wait until it's cold This is the hardest part, but you should not cut the bread while it is still hot, or it will get chewy. Wait 1 or even 2 hours before slicing it.

My experience making sourdough bread

First bread

For my first bread I followed a tutorial step-by-step, and it turned out just ok. I left it in bulk fermentation for 5 hours and I think it was not enough, I think the bread was underfermented.
Also, my starter was very young - only 7 days of life - so I think this might have affected it too.

Second bread

My second bread was better. I made it in a cold day so I assumed this would influence fermenting times, I left it in bulk fermentation for 8 hours and it grew beautifully. Maybe I could leave it a little more, but I'm not sure.

Next breads

For my next trials, I want to experiment with different flours. Also, I want to start adding some seeds and nuts in the mixture.
